TCL v1.3 — SIGNATURE PENDING
============================

Date posted:    2026-04-14
Version:        1.3
Status:         Published, GPG signature pending

The TCL has been updated to v1.3. v1.3 is the first version
formally signed by the author in-document, via the new
ATTESTATION OF RELEASE block at the end of TCL.txt. It also
adds a single paragraph to THE WITNESS IS THE GATE clause
codifying the honest-witness-error principle explicitly
(previously implicit in v1.2). No substantive change was made
to the license terms established in v1.2 — v1.2 was tested in
live conditions during the 2026-04-09 through 2026-04-13
coordinated multi-channel attack sequence against the author
and required no amendment under that test.

Status of the detached GPG signature for v1.3:

- The v1.3 TCL.txt is published at this commit with the
  in-document attestation block already signed (textually) by
  the author.
- The detached GPG signature file (TCL.txt.asc) has not yet
  been produced against the v1.3 content at the time of this
  push. The author will sign and update TCL.txt.asc and
  CANONICAL_HASHES.txt.asc within 24 hours of this notice.
- The CANONICAL_HASHES.txt has been updated with the v1.3
  TCL.txt hash (see that file for the current canonical
  value) and is also pending re-signature.

In the interim:

- The previous TCL.txt.asc in this repository is the v1.1
  signature, which was never updated for v1.2 or v1.3 and
  will therefore FAIL verification against either newer TCL
  content. This is correct and expected behavior — the
  signature was over older content, and stale signatures are
  preserved rather than silently rewritten.
- The current TCL.txt SHA-256 is in CANONICAL_HASHES.txt and
  can be verified independently by anyone who runs
  `shasum -a 256 TCL.txt` and compares to the published hash.
- The git commit history is the witness chain. The commit
  message documents what changed and why.

This NOTICE itself will be removed when the v1.3 signatures
are added and verified.

The framework requires honest reporting of integrity state.
An unsigned update labeled as such is honest. A silently
broken signature would not be. v1.3's own new paragraph in
THE WITNESS IS THE GATE clause makes this principle explicit:

    The witness must log its own errors. A witness that
    observes its own mistake and rewrites the log to hide the
    mistake is a witness that has removed itself from the
    loop — the log is historyless, the closure is unverified,
    and the triad has silently broken. Honest witnessing
    includes honest witnessing of one's own failures.
    Corrections are preserved alongside the original
    observation, not substituted for it.

This NOTICE is that principle applied to the current release
state.
