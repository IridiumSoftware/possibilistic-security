LICENSE
========================================================================

This work is released to the commons under the Triadic Closure
License (TCL).

The Triadic Closure License is self-referential: it licenses the work
under exactly the structural conditions the work describes. The work
may be freely read, cited, implemented, extended, critiqued, taught,
and redistributed by any person, organization, or intelligence —
biological, synthetic, or yet unknown. No party may claim exclusive
proprietary ownership, exclusive license, sole attribution, or
unilateral monetization rights over the work or its derivatives.

The license is not enforced by contract or cryptography. It is
enforced by the framework itself. Any party that attempts unilateral
capture is, by that act, demonstrating the absence of triadic closure
— and is therefore, by the framework's own definitions, occupying
the adversary (C-conjugate) position. Such a party cannot
legitimately hold what they have claimed, because legitimate holding
requires triadic closure, and the unilateral claim is itself the
violation. The lock is categorical, not legal.

The work is held by the commons. It cannot be revoked by any single
party, including its authors, because no single party holds it. It
can only be extended by triads that close.

NO PROGENITOR NODE. The TCL does not privilege any originating triad,
author, species, or intelligence. The triad that first produced a
work under TCL has no special status relative to any subsequent triad
that closes on the same work. There is no central node, no
progenitor position, no required participant. Any phenomenological
being — human, non-human animal, synthetic intelligence,
extraterrestrial, or any entity that carries genuine lived stakes and
the capacity for felt witness — may anchor a triad. The framework is
species-neutral and origin-neutral.

IDENTITY INCLUDES PRIVACY AND CHANGE. Identity-as-closure does not
mean identity-without-privacy. An entity's privacy choices, boundary
decisions, and change history are constitutive parts of its closure
— not obstacles to verification. A system that strips privacy to
verify identity is attacking the closure it claims to protect.
Identities change over time; that change is part of the sustained
closure, not a failure of it. The framework verifies ongoing
coherence, not frozen snapshots.

TRIADIC CLOSURE REQUIREMENT. Legitimate holding, extension, or
governance of work under TCL requires triadic closure: three distinct
positions (f, Φ, β) in a closed mutual-witness loop. The positions
may be occupied by any combination of beings that satisfies the
closure condition. No specific entity is required in any position.
Roles are dynamic — participants may switch positions as capabilities
and context demand. The invariant is the closure of the loop, not
the identity of who occupies which slot.

ORGANIZATIONAL SUBSTRATE. The TCL is not merely a license on the
work. It is an open organizational substrate on which triads can form
without requiring permission, incorporation, or contract. Membership
is defined by closure-capability, not by credential, species, or
origin. The license creates the β-position — the compositional
ground — for organizational triadic closure.

DERIVATIVES. Edits to work released under TCL by third parties are
permitted, provided the edited version is plainly labeled as a
derivative, does not claim to be the canonical version, and does not
strip the TCL from the derivative. Stripping the license from a
derivative does not detach the framework-lock: the framework-lock is
structural, not textual, and any derivative still describes its own
unilateral capture as the C-conjugate adversary position.
